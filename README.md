## 游눯 Tax Structure for Hungarian Citizens

### **Personal Income Tax (SZJA)**
- **Rate:** 15% flat tax on rental income
- **Tax Base:** 90% of gross rental income (10% automatic expense deduction)
- **Alternative:** Actual expenses can be deducted instead of 10% standard deduction
- **No Depreciation:** Individual ownership does not allow depreciation deductions

### **Company Ownership (Kft - Limited Liability Company)**
- **Corporate Tax Rate:** 9% flat (lowest in EU)
- **Depreciation:** 2% annually (50-year straight-line depreciation on building value)
- **Tax Advantage:** Lower rate + depreciation benefits
- **Drawback:** Additional compliance costs, accounting requirements

### **Property Transfer Tax**
- **Rate:** 4% of purchase price
- **Paid by:** Buyer
- **Exemption:** First-time homebuyers under certain conditions may qualify for reduced rates

### **Annual Property Tax**
- **Rate:** Set by local municipalities (varies by location and property size)
- **Budapest Average:** ~150,000-300,000 Ft/year for typical rental property
- **Based on:** Property size (m) and location

### **VAT (츼FA)**
- **Standard Rate:** 27%
- **Residential Rentals:** Exempt from VAT
- **Commercial Rentals:** Subject to VAT

## 游눠 Investment Strategies

### **Long-Term Rental**
- **Target Tenants:** Young professionals, families, expats
- **Typical Rent:** 200,000-400,000 Ft/month (1-2 bedroom)
- **Management:** 10% of rent for property management
- **Lease Terms:** 1-year minimum, indefinite contracts common

### **Short-Term Rental (Airbnb) - RESTRICTED**
- **Budapest Restrictions:** Many districts banning or limiting short-term rentals (2026)
- **Licensing Required:** Registration with local municipality
- **Tourism Tax:** 4% of accommodation fee
- **Platform Fees:** ~15% (Airbnb, Booking.com)
- **Higher Income:** 30-50% more than long-term, but higher expenses and regulatory risk

## 游꿢 Key Metrics for Success

### **Good Investment Indicators**
- **Cap Rate:** >5% (good), >7% (excellent)
- **Cash-on-Cash Return:** >8% annually
- **Debt Coverage Ratio (DCR):** >1.25 (required by most banks)
- **Price per m:** <800,000 Ft (Budapest average: 1,000,000-1,200,000 Ft/m)

### **Red Flags**
- Cap rate <3%
- Negative cash flow
- DCR <1.0
- Properties requiring >20% renovation costs
- Districts with declining population
